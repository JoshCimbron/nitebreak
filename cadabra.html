Cadabra

Bring stories to life with magical sound effects

The Challenge

Bedtime stories just got a little more magical. Cadabra encourages children to read with sound effects. Create your own sound effects or choose from an extensive library to bring your stories to life. Link your sounds to a word in the story and listen to them trigger when the word is read. For this personal project we handled Cadabra’s design and prototyping.

Identifying user goals and needs

Cadabra’s demographic is geared towards children new to reading and their parents. Before we get to building the app we like to kick off the design process by getting to know our users. That way we can build awesome experiences just for them!

From Blueprint to Prototype

Designing an app like Cadabra can quickly become overwhelming. That’s why we start by mapping out the app’s information architecture. This helps us get a feel for what features and screens we need to prioritize.

A user flow is a great tool to map out the app’s structure. The user flow helps us prioritize the most important features to design and build for our prototype.

We begin to flesh out the most important structural elements of the interface with lo-fi wireframes.

A moodboard helps keep the visual direction of the project consistent and focused.

We make things pop by incorporating colors, font families, and other stylistic choices to build high fidelity wireframes.

Get Creating Fast

Your time and effort should be invested in creating awesome content, not in learning complicated software. Cadabra makes customizing your favorite stories fast and easy.

Explore an extensive library of books and save your favorites to read and customize.

A simple menu and intuitive touch controls makes finding and adding sound effects easy.

Can't find a sound effect? Record and save your own.

Everyone needs a little help sometimes. A short and engaging tutorial helps new users customize their first story.

What we learned

Creating an app from scratch is tough work. If we had a time machine we’d tighten the scope of the project and hone in on one or two of the most important features that define the app. It’s easy to get caught up in all the stuff the app should have. But it’s the features that our users love the most, like customizing stories and creating sound effects, that deserve the most love and attention.
